About Me

I received my B.S. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the Ohio State University, Columbus, OH, in 2005. I was an NSF Graduate Research Fellow and received my Ph.D. degree in Biomedical Engineering from Duke University in 2013. After defending, I completed a 15-month postdoc as a Whitaker International Program scholar in the Electrical Engineering Department at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU) in Lyngby, Denmark. I have since started working as a Research Scientist with Gregg Trahey at Duke Univeristy.

My research interests include synthetic aperture ultrasound imaging, coherence imaging, vector flow imaging, GPU computing, and acoustic radiation force impulse imaging.
